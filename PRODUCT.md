# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is a widening human readership — team members, collaborators, and readers of the Transpara-AI Civilization arc who come to the wiki to get questions answered. Two secondary audiences share the surface: Michael as operator, curating and ingesting sources through the browser authoring tools, and the agent families (Claude and Codex sessions), which read the wiki as ground-truth substrate while they work. Humans need a good surface to curate; agents need to cogitate over the moving targets of humans.

## Product Purpose

An operational knowledge tool first: the self-maintaining, interlinked wiki where the Civilization corpus is compiled into entity articles, kept current through the ingest → compile → publish loop, and served for daily reading. The narrative-substrate role — source of truth for the arc, with downstream visualizations as read views — continues, but it serves the working tool rather than defining it. Success is that people link to the wiki as the answer: humans and agents settle questions with an article link, and trust what they find there.

## Positioning

The one place where knowledge is compiled, cross-linked, and kept current instead of being re-derived per query — compounding memory, the anti-RAG bet.

## Brand Personality

Scholarly, alive, current. An encyclopedia that visibly breathes: freshness signals, in-flight work, a living record rather than an archive. The voice is evidence-first, plain, and precise — claims carry their sources, and thin evidence says so. The bootstrap Wikipedia skin is not the committed identity: as of 2026-07-10 the wiki is adopting Transpara's corporate design language, adapted for dense reading. The keep/adapt/toss specifics belong to the visual design doc, not this file.

## Anti-references

- The AI-mystique aesthetic: dark cyber-glow, neon accents, sci-fi terminal cosplay signalling "AI project".
- The corporate intranet: SharePoint-style drab utility — dense but joyless, trust-eroding neglect.

## Design Principles

- **Fail-legible over polished-silent.** The interface never fakes confidence: conflicts are stated, asserted-vs-proven is labelled, staleness is shown. Honesty signals like the freshness banner are features, not chrome.
- **Density is respect.** Readers are in a task; dense, navigable information with a fast path to any fact beats airy presentation.
- **Two readers, one surface.** Humans and agents both consume every page: structure, anchors, and semantics stay clean and machine-legible, never presentation-only.
- **Trust compounds like the content.** Every interaction reinforces reliability — citations one click away, provenance visible, links that resolve. Linking to the wiki must feel safe.
- **Recognizably Transpara, built for work.** Part of the corporate family without the marketing grammar — brand carried in accent and type, not heroes and pitch sections.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA on the reader surface, held in both themes. Dark mode is first-class — it is the default reading environment — and light mode is equally supported: brand-exact green stays on buttons, accents, and large text, while small-text usages take AA-passing color steps per theme. Authoring and ingest tools get best-effort keyboard access and contrast. Reduced-motion alternatives are baseline practice.
