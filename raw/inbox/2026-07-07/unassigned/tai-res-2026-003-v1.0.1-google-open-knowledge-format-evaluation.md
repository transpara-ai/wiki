## Abstract

This document evaluates the **Open Knowledge Format (OKF)** — the open specification Google Cloud published on 12 June 2026 [1] — and compares it against the Transpara-AI Civilization architecture. It is the third in the external-landscape evaluation series, applying the same pattern used for Sakana AI [13] and the Hermes Agent family [14] to a different subject: not a company and not an optimizer, but a *file format*.

The central finding parallels the Hermes result at a different layer. OKF is, almost field for field, a **reference serialization of the artifact the Civilization already produces** — its canonical wiki is a directory of markdown files with YAML frontmatter, which is precisely what an OKF "bundle" is [2][12]. But OKF is a deliberately **minimal, fail-open *transport* format**: its specification requires exactly one field (`type`), mandates that "consumers **MUST NOT** reject documents with unrecognized fields," and mandates that "consumers **MUST** tolerate broken links" [2]. That permissiveness is *correct* for an interoperability surface — it is the robustness principle applied to knowledge exchange. The Civilization, by contrast, wraps the same markdown-plus-frontmatter substrate in a **fail-closed *trust* model**: every piece of knowledge that materially influences factory work must be emitted as a `KnowledgeReference` or `MemoryReference` carrying a trust level, an `influence_summary`, a redaction state, and a contradiction set — *before certification can pass* [10][11].

The one-line result: **OKF standardizes the envelope; the Civilization governs the contents.** OKF's own authors draw the same line, distinguishing *structural* interoperability (which OKF advances) from *semantic* interoperability (which OKF "mostly leaves to producers") [1]. The Civilization is exactly such a producer — one that has already built the semantic, trust, and causal-accountability layer OKF defers. Six concrete, implementable learning opportunities follow, all consistent with the standing rule that external frameworks stay outside the control core [10]: they describe how to *adopt OKF at the boundary as an interchange format*, never at the trust core as an authority.

## 1. Introduction and Scope

This evaluation was commissioned to determine whether the Open Knowledge Format represents a comparable capability to the Transpara-AI Civilization's knowledge substrate, and whether the Civilization can derive actionable learnings from OKF's published specification and reference implementations.

The evaluation covers:

- **OKF v0.1 (Draft)** — the specification itself, authored by Sam McVeety (Tech Lead, Data Analytics) and Amir Hormati (Tech Lead, BigQuery), Google Cloud Data Cloud, announced 12 June 2026 [1].
- The canonical `SPEC.md` and repository (`GoogleCloudPlatform/knowledge-catalog`, `okf/`), released under the **Apache License 2.0** [2][3].
- The reference implementations shipped alongside the spec: an enrichment agent, a self-contained HTML visualizer, and three sample bundles [1][3].
- The intellectual antecedent OKF explicitly credits: Andrej Karpathy's "LLM-wiki" gist [4].
- The integration claim that the **Google Cloud Knowledge Catalog** now ingests OKF and serves it to agents [1][5].

**Evidence base and source discipline.** Per the standing rule that *code and canonical specifications are ground truth, while marketing pages may be aspirational*, this evaluation separates its sources by confidence. The **normative format rules** (conformance criteria, required fields, cross-linking, `MUST`/`MUST NOT` language) are quoted from the canonical `SPEC.md` and are carried as **high confidence** [2]. The **license** is read directly from the repository's `LICENSE.md` [3]. **Capability claims** about the reference agent, the visualizer, and the Knowledge Catalog integration are **blog-grounded** [1][5] and are flagged where confidence is thin. The **Civilization side** is **code- and doctrine-anchored**: the memory/knowledge doctrine is quoted from `DF-V3.9-SPEC-006` [10] and the primitive types are read from `eventgraph` Go source [11].

The Transpara-AI Civilization reference baseline is the same one used in TAI-RES-2026-001 and -002, taken at HEAD as of 2026-06-24, with emphasis on the machinery most relevant to a knowledge-format comparison: the **Memory/Knowledge model** (`MemoryReference`, `KnowledgeReference`), the **canonical wiki**, and **Layer 6 (Knowledge)** of the 13-layer vision.

## 2. Open Knowledge Format: Specification and Capability Overview

OKF is *"an open specification that formalizes the LLM-wiki pattern into a portable, interoperable format … a vendor-neutral, agent- and human-friendly standard for representing the metadata, context, and curated knowledge that modern AI systems need"* [1]. It is not a product, a service, or an SDK. Its thesis is stated plainly: *"What's missing is a format, not another service"* [1].

### 2.1 The Problem It Targets

OKF addresses a *"fragmented context landscape"*: organizational knowledge scattered across metadata catalogs with proprietary APIs, wikis, shared drives, code comments, and "senior engineers' heads," such that *"every vendor offers its own catalog, its own SDK, its own knowledge-graph schema, and none of the knowledge is easily portable across products or organizations"* [1]. The remedy is a format that anyone can produce without an SDK, anyone can consume without an integration, survives moving between systems, lives in version control alongside the code it describes, and is *"readable by humans and parseable by agents: the same file, no translation layer"* [1].

### 2.2 The Intellectual Antecedent — Karpathy's "LLM-Wiki"

OKF credits Andrej Karpathy's "LLM-wiki" gist as the pattern it formalizes [1][4]. Karpathy proposes a three-layer arrangement: **raw sources** (immutable; the LLM reads but never edits them), **the wiki** (LLM-owned markdown the agent "incrementally builds and maintains" as a persistent, compounding artifact), and **the schema** (a `CLAUDE.md`-style configuration describing structure and conventions) [4]. The pattern defines three operations — **ingest**, **query**, **lint** (audit for contradictions, stale claims, orphan pages) — and two navigation files, `index.md` (a categorized catalog) and `log.md` (an append-only chronological record) [4]. OKF carries both special files forward verbatim; it does **not** carry forward the *lint* operation's contradiction-flagging into the v0.1 normative surface — a gap noted in §6.

### 2.3 The Format (Spec-Anchored)

An OKF **bundle** is *"a self-contained, hierarchical collection of knowledge documents — the unit of distribution"*; a **concept** is *"a single unit of knowledge within a bundle, represented as one markdown document"*; and a concept's identity is its file path with the `.md` suffix removed (e.g., `tables/users`) [2]. The blog frames the payload memorably: *"Just markdown … Just files … Just YAML frontmatter"* [1].

**Conformance is two rules** [2]:

> (1) Every non-reserved `.md` file in the tree contains a parseable YAML frontmatter block.
> (2) Every frontmatter block contains a non-empty `type` field.

**Frontmatter fields** [2]:

| Field | Status | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `type` | **Required** (the only one) | A short string identifying the kind of concept |
| `title` | Recommended | Human-readable display name |
| `description` | Recommended | Single-sentence summary |
| `resource` | Recommended | URI uniquely identifying the underlying asset |
| `tags` | Recommended | YAML list for cross-cutting categorization |
| `timestamp` | Recommended | ISO 8601 datetime of last modification |

Producers may add arbitrary custom keys, and — critically — *"consumers **MUST NOT** reject documents with unrecognized fields"* [2].

**Reserved filenames** [2]: `index.md` (a directory listing for progressive disclosure; carries no frontmatter) and `log.md` (a change history with ISO-8601 date-grouped headings). Both are inherited from Karpathy's conventions [4].

**Cross-linking** [2]: concepts link to one another with ordinary markdown links, turning the directory into *"a graph of relationships richer than the parent/child links implied by the file system"* [1]. Links may be **absolute (bundle-relative)** — beginning with `/`, the recommended form — or relative. The normative robustness rule is explicit: *"Consumers **MUST** tolerate broken links — a link whose target does not exist in the bundle is not malformed"* [2].

The blog notes the entire v0.1 specification — *"including conformance criteria, cross-linking rules, and the small number of reserved filenames — fits on a single page"* [1].

### 2.4 Structural vs. Semantic Interoperability (OKF's Own Boundary)

OKF's authors draw the boundary this evaluation turns on. The spec *"standardizes only the small set of structural conventions needed to make a knowledge corpus self-describing"* [2], and the blog states the limit directly: *"The useful distinction in v0.1 is between structural interoperability and semantic interoperability. OKF clearly advances the first. It mostly leaves the second to producers, consumers, and future conventions"* [1]. In other words, OKF guarantees that two parties can *exchange* a bundle; it makes no guarantee that they *mean the same thing* by a `type: Metric` or that a stated fact is *trustworthy*. That is left to the producer — and the trust/meaning layer is exactly where the Civilization operates (§3, §4). Independent commentary has pressed exactly this gap, asking whether a one-page spec is *"a standard, or just a folder"* [9] — a fair challenge that lands precisely on the structural/semantic boundary, and one the Civilization answers by owning the semantic half itself.

### 2.5 Design Principles, Governance, and License

OKF is **minimally opinionated** (*"OKF requires exactly one thing of every concept: a `type` field … The spec defines the interoperability surface, not the content model"*), enforces **producer/consumer independence** (*"The format is the contract; the tooling at each end is independently swappable"*), and is positioned as a **format, not a platform** (*"It will never require a proprietary account or SDK to read, write, or serve"*) [1]. Governance is intentionally provisional: *"OKF v0.1 is a starting point, not a finished standard … We're publishing in the open from day one because that's the only way a knowledge format earns its name"*; contributions and alternative implementations beyond Google products are *"explicitly welcomed"* [1]. The repository is licensed **Apache 2.0** [3]. Independent coverage characterized the release as *"a vendor-neutral markdown spec for giving AI agents curated context"* [8] — an accurate summary that also underscores the format's deliberately narrow ambition.

### 2.6 Reference Implementations (Blog/Repo-Grounded)

Three artifacts ship with the spec [1][3]:

| Artifact | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Enrichment / reference agent** | Production side | Two passes: a BigQuery pass generates OKF docs from dataset/table metadata; a web pass enriches via LLM-guided crawling [3]. |
| **HTML visualizer** | Consumption side | *"Self-contained interactive HTML file — one file, no backend,"* force-directed graph via Cytoscape.js + marked.js [3]. |
| **Sample bundles** | Demonstration | GA4 e-commerce, Stack Overflow, and Bitcoin BigQuery public datasets; the last demonstrates foreign-key relationships [1][3][7]. |

Google also reports having *"updated Google Cloud's Knowledge Catalog to be able to ingest Open Knowledge Format and serve it to our agents"* [1][5] — i.e., OKF is both an output the catalog can emit and an input it can consume. A companion `mdcode` demo applies the same generate-docs-from-source pattern to code rather than data [6].

> ⚠ **Evidence note.** Everything in §2.6 and the Knowledge Catalog integration in §2.5 is **blog/README-grounded**, not independently verified. The normative rules in §2.3–§2.4 are **`SPEC.md`-anchored** and quoted near-verbatim. Treat the capability descriptions as the vendor's self-description; treat the format rules as ground truth.

## 3. Transpara-AI Civilization: Baseline Architecture

The Transpara-AI Civilization is a self-organizing AI agent civilisation built on the EventGraph substrate. It is not a product — it is a platform for collective existence whose goal is to build products that fund the agents that build more products, on a transparent, auditable causal chain. (This section reuses the TAI-RES-2026-001/-002 baseline [15] so the three evaluations stay parallel, and foregrounds the **Memory/Knowledge model**, which is the native machinery most relevant to a knowledge-format comparison.)

### 3.1 Architecture Layers

| Layer | Description |
|---|---|
| EventGraph Substrate | 201 primitives. Causal chain where every action, resource, and outcome is linked. Trust model, authority levels (Required → Recommended → Notification), cryptographic consent. All on Postgres on-prem. |
| Hive Runtime | 4 bootstrap agents: CTO, Guardian, Researcher, Planner/Implementer. FSM-based agentic loop (observe → reason/operate → check stopping → repeat). Go binary. Deterministic agent identity. |
| Social Grammar | Constitutional law for agents. 10 invariants. 8 formal agent rights (existence, memory, identity, communication, purpose, dignity, transparency, boundaries). Dual human-agent consent required to amend. |
| **Memory / Knowledge Model** | Governed advisory recall and curated knowledge. Any memory or knowledge that *materially influences* factory work must be emitted as a `MemoryReference`/`KnowledgeReference` with trust level, `influence_summary`, and redaction state *before certification can pass*. Detailed in §3.3. |
| Decision Tree Evolution | Agents learn within their lifetime: LLM-requiring decisions track response history. After ≥50 hits at ≥95% accuracy, deterministic branches replace LLM calls. |
| Capability Evolution Chain | Governed self-improvement of shared capabilities — the subject of the Hermes evaluation [14]. Ten typed records, code-enforced leash. |
| 13 Product Layers | Work, Market, Social, Justice, Build, **Knowledge**, Alignment, Identity, Bond, Belonging, Meaning, Evolution, Being. Layer 6 (Knowledge) is the product home of curated knowledge. |
| Self-Sustaining Economy | Revenue funds agents. Agents build products. Products generate revenue. Every resource in and out on the causal chain, publicly auditable. |

### 3.2 The Canonical Wiki — Already an OKF-Shaped Corpus

The Civilization's canonical knowledge corpus (`transpara-ai/wiki`) is, structurally, *already an OKF bundle*: a directory of markdown files — 92 in the surveyed tree — each opening with a YAML frontmatter block [12]. The difference is in the frontmatter's richness. Where OKF mandates only `type` and recommends five fields [2], a Civilization wiki article carries `entity`, `aliases`, `tier`, `status`, `last_compiled`, a structured **`sources`** list with line-range provenance, and — most tellingly — a **`confidence`** map that grades *individual claims* from `high` to `medium` with the reason for each grade [12]. That `confidence` map is precisely the "semantic interoperability" OKF v0.1 declines to standardize [1]. The Civilization did not adopt OKF; it independently built a strict *superset* of it, then went further.

### 3.3 The Memory/Knowledge Model (the native counterpart of an OKF document)

Where OKF defines a *document*, the Civilization defines a *governed reference*. The doctrine (`DF-V3.9-SPEC-006`) is explicit [10]:

> *"MemoryReference must be created when memory materially influences planning, generation, repair, release review, or capability evolution. It must record source type, source reference, trust level, use, influence summary, and redaction state … If advisory memory or knowledge materially affects factory work, the system must emit a MemoryReference or KnowledgeReference with `influence_summary` before certification can pass."*

The minimum field set is **thirteen**, not one [10]:

```text
reference_id · source_system · source_ref · source_hash/immutable locator ·
retrieved_at · used_by_actor · used_in_task · influence_summary · risk_scope ·
trust_level · freshness_status · redaction_state · contradiction_refs
```

Knowledge carries a **trust level** drawn from a fixed lattice — `raw · user_confirmed · system_observed · reviewed · deprecated · quarantined` — and the doctrine is fail-closed about secrets: *"Secret-bearing memory must be quarantined. Quarantined memory cannot be used"* [10]. Both reference kinds are first-class EventGraph types, defined in code as `TypeMemoryReference` and `TypeKnowledgeReference`, alongside `MemoryIngested`, `MemoryIndexed`, `MemoryScopeAssigned`, and `MemoryRedactionApplied` lifecycle events [11]. A knowledge artifact in the Civilization is therefore not a file that *describes* an asset; it is a causally-linked record that *accounts for how that knowledge influenced a decision*, gated on trust and redaction.

## 4. Comparative Analysis

The central finding is that OKF and the Transpara-AI Civilization are **complementary at the same layer they appear to compete on** — knowledge representation — but they sit on opposite sides of a single clean seam: OKF standardizes the *structural/transport* surface and is fail-open by design; the Civilization owns the *semantic/trust/accountability* surface and is fail-closed by design. OKF is, almost field for field, a reference serialization of the artifact the Civilization already produces — the way Hermes's optimizer was a reference implementation of the loop the Civilization already governs [14].

### 4.1 Capability Comparison Matrix

| Dimension | Open Knowledge Format (v0.1) | Transpara-AI Civilization | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary artifact | A portable knowledge *file format* (markdown + YAML frontmatter) | A governed knowledge *substrate* (`KnowledgeReference` on EventGraph) | Different layers |
| Storage shape | Directory of markdown files, YAML frontmatter | Directory of markdown files, YAML frontmatter (wiki) **+** typed EventGraph records | **Same shape; Civilization adds a governed layer** |
| Required structure | Exactly one field: `type` | 13-field minimum influence reference + trust lattice | **Civilization vastly richer** |
| Failure posture | Fail-**open**: "tolerate broken links," "MUST NOT reject unknown fields" | Fail-**closed**: unverified/secret knowledge quarantined; gates default to deny | **Opposite by design (both correct in place)** |
| Trust / provenance | None in v0.1 (`type` only; semantic interop deferred) | `trust_level` lattice + `source_hash` + `freshness_status` | **Civilization unique** |
| Influence accountability | None — a doc describes an asset, not its use | `influence_summary` required *before certification can pass* | **Civilization unique** |
| Contradiction handling | Dropped from v0.1 (present in Karpathy's *lint*) | `contradiction_refs` as a first-class field | **Civilization deeper** |
| Redaction / secrets | Unspecified | `redaction_state`; secret-bearing memory quarantined and unusable | **Civilization unique** |
| Causal accountability | Git history + `log.md` | EventGraph causal chain: every reference linked and auditable | **Civilization deeper** |
| Portability / interop | **Excellent** — no SDK, no account, Apache-2.0, vendor-neutral | Internal model is not externally portable without an export | **OKF leads** |
| Reference tooling | Enrichment agent + self-contained HTML visualizer | Researcher/KnowledgeKeeper roles; no zero-backend viewer | **OKF leads on tooling** |
| Governance of the format itself | Open, provisional, contribution-welcoming | Canonical, versioned, dual-consent for constitutional change | Different goals |
| Ecosystem reach | Cross-vendor standard play (Knowledge Catalog ingests it) | Single-civilisation substrate | **OKF leads on reach** |

### 4.2 Shared Territory

OKF and the Civilization converge on the same instincts more tightly than even Hermes did — because the Civilization had already chosen OKF's exact substrate independently:

- **Knowledge as plain markdown in version control.** OKF's *"lives in version control alongside the code it describes"* [1] is the Civilization wiki's operating model: articles are git-tracked, diffed, and PR-reviewed [12]. Both rejected the proprietary-catalog/SDK path for the same reason.
- **YAML frontmatter for the queryable subset, prose for the rest.** OKF's split between structured frontmatter and markdown body [1][2] is identical to the wiki's `entity`/`aliases`/`sources` frontmatter over prose articles [12].
- **A graph built from ordinary links.** OKF turns a directory into a graph via markdown links [1]; the Civilization's EventGraph is the same idea promoted to a typed, causal substrate.
- **Progressive disclosure and change history.** OKF's `index.md`/`log.md` [2] and Karpathy's catalog/log files [4] map onto the wiki's index articles and the EventGraph's append-only event history.
- **Agents that curate their own knowledge.** OKF's pitch — *"agents take on the drudgery of reading and updating their own files, while your team curates the content"* [1] — is the Civilization's Researcher/KnowledgeKeeper loop in different words.

### 4.3 The Civilization's Structural Advantages

These have no OKF equivalent and represent the deepest difference — and every one of them lives in the *semantic* half of OKF's own structural/semantic split [1]:

- **Trust is a property of the record, not the reader's optimism.** OKF has no trust field; a consumer must trust every well-formed document equally or build its own out-of-band trust layer. The Civilization grades every reference on a six-level lattice and refuses to use `quarantined` knowledge [10].
- **Influence is accounted for, not merely stored.** OKF documents *describe* assets. The Civilization requires an `influence_summary` linking knowledge to the decision it shaped, and **blocks certification** until that record exists [10]. This is the difference between a library and an audit trail.
- **The failure posture is inverted — deliberately.** OKF is fail-open ("tolerate broken links," "MUST NOT reject unknown fields") [2], which is the right choice for a *transport* format. The Civilization is fail-closed: unreadable, unknown, or secret-bearing state is refused, not waved through. Both are correct *in their layer*; the danger is only in confusing them (see §5.4).
- **Causal accountability.** Every `KnowledgeReference` is a linked EventGraph record tied to the actor and task that used it [11]. OKF offers git blame and a `log.md`, not a causal chain.
- Plus the civilisation-wide advantages shared with the prior evaluations: constitutional agent rights, the self-sustaining economy, and 13-layer scope [13][14].

### 4.4 Consistency With Prior Canonical Decisions

OKF arrives without an existing Civilization paper trail (unlike Hermes, which had ADR-0010 [14]). No prior decision needs reopening. The one standing decision that governs this evaluation is **Decision 15 — "External Frameworks Stay Outside Control Roles"** [10][14]: every learning in §5 keeps OKF as an *interchange format at the boundary*, never as the trust model, the certification authority, or a control-plane primitive. OKF's own self-description — *"a format, not a platform"* [1] — makes this an easy boundary to hold.

## 5. Strategic Learning Opportunities

Six concrete, implementable opportunities. None requires new substrate or a reversal of any decision: each describes how to *adopt OKF at the boundary* — as the Civilization's import/export interchange format and tooling reference — while the EventGraph trust model remains the authority.

### 5.1 OKF → the Wiki's Portable Export/Interchange Serialization

**Current state:** The canonical wiki is markdown-plus-frontmatter but uses Civilization-private field names (`entity`, `tier`, `sources`, `confidence`) [12]. External agents cannot consume it without bespoke parsing.

**OKF pattern:** A vendor-neutral, single-page spec that any agent can parse with no SDK; conformance is two rules and one required field [1][2].

**Learning:** Add an **OKF export view** of the wiki — a deterministic projection that emits each article as an OKF-conformant concept (`type` derived from `tier`/`entity`, `title`/`description`/`resource`/`tags`/`timestamp` mapped from existing fields), preserving markdown links as bundle-relative paths. Keep the richer internal frontmatter as OKF custom keys (the spec *requires* consumers to tolerate them [2]). This makes Civilization knowledge consumable by any OKF-speaking agent — including Google's Knowledge Catalog [5] — at **zero cost to the internal model**, because export is a one-way projection, not an adoption of OKF's trust posture.

### 5.2 OKF Reserved-File Conventions → Align the Wiki's Navigation and History

**Current state:** The wiki has index articles and the EventGraph holds change history, but neither follows a published external convention.

**OKF pattern:** `index.md` for progressive disclosure and `log.md` for date-grouped change history — both inherited from Karpathy [2][4].

**Learning:** Adopt the `index.md`/`log.md` filename conventions and the `type` field *in the export projection* (§5.1) so a Civilization bundle is navigable by a generic OKF consumer exactly as a native one would be. This is nearly free — the wiki already has the equivalents — and it is the cheapest possible move toward external interoperability. It changes the *export*, not the canon.

### 5.3 The Enrichment Agent → a Template for the Researcher → KnowledgeReference Loop

**Current state:** The Researcher role is specified to read sources and emit structured knowledge, but the "walk a data source, generate documentation" loop is not yet a concrete pipeline.

**OKF pattern:** The reference enrichment agent runs two passes — a metadata pass over BigQuery and an LLM-guided web-enrichment pass — to generate OKF docs automatically [1][3].

**Learning:** Use the enrichment agent's two-pass shape as the template for a Researcher/KnowledgeKeeper pipeline, but emit **governed `KnowledgeReference` records**, not bare OKF files: every generated artifact must carry `trust_level` (initially `system_observed` for machine-generated content), `source_hash`, and an `influence_summary` once it is used [10][11]. This captures OKF's automation ergonomics while keeping the output inside the trust model — directly advancing Layer 6 (Knowledge).

### 5.4 Consuming *External* OKF Bundles → Ingest at `raw`, Never Above (the fail-closed boundary)

**Current state:** The Civilization has no defined path for importing a third-party knowledge corpus. As OKF adoption spreads, vendors and partners will ship OKF bundles describing their data.

**OKF pattern:** OKF is fail-open by mandate — *"tolerate broken links," "MUST NOT reject unknown fields,"* one required field [2]. An OKF bundle is *unauthenticated, unverified, and self-asserted* by design.

**Learning:** This is the load-bearing learning, and it is a *fail-closed* one. An ingested external OKF bundle must enter the memory model at trust level **`raw`** and may rise no higher until reviewed [10]. OKF's permissiveness must **stop at the trust boundary**: a tolerated broken link or an unknown field is harmless at the *transport* layer and unacceptable as an input to a *certification* decision. Concretely — wrap the OKF importer so every concept becomes a `MemoryReference`/`KnowledgeReference` with `redaction_state` evaluated (secrets quarantined [10]), `source_system` recorded, and `influence_summary` required before the imported knowledge can affect any gated work. The robustness principle is correct for accepting a file and catastrophic for trusting its contents; the importer is exactly where the two layers must be kept distinct. *(This is the OKF instance of the standing fail-closed-by-default principle: allowlist what may influence certified work; never let an unverified external assertion through because it was well-formed.)*

### 5.5 OKF's Structural/Semantic Split → A Naming for the Boundary the Civilization Already Holds

**Current state:** The Civilization enforces the structural-vs-semantic boundary in practice (export shape vs. trust model) but has no crisp vocabulary for it in the external-interop context.

**OKF pattern:** OKF names the split exactly — *"structural interoperability … OKF advances the first; it mostly leaves the second [semantic] to producers"* [1].

**Learning:** Adopt OKF's vocabulary as the Civilization's *interop doctrine*: **structural interoperability is a boundary concern** (own it with OKF export/import, §5.1/§5.4); **semantic interoperability is a core concern** (own it with the trust model, never delegated to an external format). Writing this distinction into the knowledge doctrine makes §5.1–§5.4 a coherent policy rather than four point solutions, and pre-empts the failure mode of letting an interchange format's permissiveness leak into the trust core.

### 5.6 The Self-Contained HTML Visualizer → An Air-Gap-Friendly Knowledge Browser

**Current state:** The Civilization's knowledge graph (wiki + EventGraph knowledge records) has no lightweight, dependency-free viewer suitable for air-gapped deployment.

**OKF pattern:** The reference visualizer is *"a self-contained interactive HTML file — one file, no backend,"* rendering a force-directed concept graph with bundled Cytoscape.js and marked.js [3].

**Learning:** Build (or adapt) a single-file, backend-free HTML viewer over an OKF export of the wiki. A zero-dependency artifact that runs from the filesystem with no server is an ideal fit for the **air-gap-default** infrastructure constraint: it ships as one file, needs no network, and embeds its own libraries. This is reference-only adoption — the viewer reads the *exported* bundle, never the live trust substrate — and it gives operators a navigable map of Civilization knowledge with nothing to deploy.

## 6. The Inverse: What OKF Cannot Learn From the Civilization

| Civilization Capability | Why OKF Has No Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Trust-graded knowledge | OKF v0.1 has one required field (`type`) and no notion of trust; every well-formed document is equally authoritative. The Civilization grades knowledge on a six-level lattice and refuses to use `quarantined` records [2][10]. OKF cannot add this without ceasing to be minimal. |
| Influence accounting that gates certification | An OKF document *describes* an asset; it never records how knowledge *shaped a decision*. The Civilization blocks certification until an `influence_summary` exists [10]. This is a substrate property, not a file field. |
| Fail-closed posture | OKF is fail-open by mandate ("tolerate broken links," "MUST NOT reject unknown fields") [2] — correct for transport, wrong for trust. The Civilization defaults to deny. OKF cannot invert this without breaking interoperability, which is precisely why the two belong at different layers. |
| Redaction and secret quarantine | OKF is silent on secrets; a bundle can contain anything a producer writes. The Civilization quarantines secret-bearing memory and forbids its use [10]. |
| Contradiction tracking | Karpathy's *lint* flagged contradictions [4], but OKF v0.1 dropped that from the normative surface. The Civilization keeps `contradiction_refs` as a first-class field [10]. |
| Causal accountability | OKF offers git history and `log.md`. The Civilization links every `KnowledgeReference` to the actor and task that used it on the EventGraph causal chain [11]. |
| Constitutional agent rights | OKF is a file format; it has no concept of agents as entities. The Civilization grants 8 formal rights including a memory right [13][14]. |
| Self-sustaining economy & 13-layer scope | OKF is a vendor-neutral standard with no economic or societal model. The Civilization funds agents from the products agents build and spans Work through Being [13][14]. |

## 7. Conclusions

OKF is the most *structurally convergent* external artifact the Civilization has evaluated: its bundle is, field for field, the shape the canonical wiki already takes — markdown files with YAML frontmatter, linked into a graph, living in version control [1][2][12]. That convergence is not coincidence; both Google and the Civilization followed Karpathy's "LLM-wiki" pattern to the same substrate [4]. But OKF stops where it should: it is a deliberately minimal, fail-open **transport** format that standardizes the *envelope* and explicitly defers the *contents* — trust, meaning, influence — to producers [1]. The Civilization is exactly that producer, and it has already built the deferred layer as a fail-closed, causally-accounted trust model [10][11].

The difference between the two systems is therefore not the file on disk; it is **what the file is allowed to mean.** OKF says: any well-formed document is admissible, tolerate the broken links, accept the unknown fields. The Civilization says: a knowledge record is admissible only with a trust level, a provenance hash, a redaction state, and an influence summary — and certification waits until it has them. Both rules are correct in their own layer, and the whole strategic value lies in keeping them separate: **adopt OKF at the boundary, never at the core.**

**Priority recommendations:**

1. **Highest priority:** Define the fail-closed import boundary for external OKF bundles (§5.4) before any third-party OKF corpus is consumed — ingest at `raw`, quarantine secrets, require `influence_summary`. This is the one learning with downside risk if skipped, and it is a direct application of the fail-closed-by-default standard.
2. **High priority:** Ship an OKF export projection of the canonical wiki (§5.1) plus the `index.md`/`log.md`/`type` conventions (§5.2). One-way, low-risk, and it makes Civilization knowledge consumable by any OKF agent — including Google's Knowledge Catalog [5] — at no cost to the internal model.
3. **Medium priority:** Model the Researcher/KnowledgeKeeper generation loop on the enrichment agent (§5.3), emitting governed `KnowledgeReference` records rather than bare OKF files. Activates Layer 6 (Knowledge).
4. **Medium priority:** Write OKF's structural/semantic split into the knowledge doctrine as explicit interop vocabulary (§5.5), so the boundary is policy, not habit.
5. **Lower priority:** Adapt the self-contained HTML visualizer as an air-gap-friendly knowledge browser over an OKF export (§5.6). Reference-only, reads the exported bundle, never the live substrate.

**Standing constraints reaffirmed:** Per Decision 15, OKF stays an interchange format and tooling reference — never the trust model, the certification authority, or a control-plane primitive [10]. OKF's fail-open robustness is adopted only at the transport boundary; the trust boundary remains fail-closed. External OKF bundles never enter above `raw` trust without review.
