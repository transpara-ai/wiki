# Google Open Knowledge Format Capability Evaluation

**Google Open Knowledge Format Capability Evaluation is the third released internal external-landscape research artifact — a read that finds Google's OKF and the [[the-civilization|Transpara-AI Civilization]] converging on the same structural shape (markdown files with YAML frontmatter) while occupying different trust layers: OKF standardizes the envelope; the Civilization governs the contents.**

## Placement

This report belongs under Civilization external-landscape research because the
research question concerns wiki/corpus structure, agent-readable knowledge,
provenance, and the boundary between structural interchange and governed
semantic trust. It is not Transpara LLC Platform competitive intelligence.

It is research evidence. It is not accepted doctrine, not production behavior,
and not authority to consume third-party OKF bundles above raw trust.

## What Changed with the Research

The evaluation (TAI-RES-2026-003, most recently v1.0.1 — a classification
correction for the record) reads Google's Open Knowledge Format as a
boundary/interchange pattern for the Civilization knowledge substrate and finds
the two **convergent in structure, divergent in trust**:

| Layer | Google OKF | Transpara-AI Civilization |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary role | open transport/interchange format | governed knowledge and causal-accountability substrate |
| File substrate | markdown files with YAML frontmatter | markdown wiki plus richer frontmatter and source/confidence discipline |
| Failure posture | fail-open for portability and broken-link tolerance | fail-closed for trusted influence on factory work |
| Semantic trust | deferred to producers and consumers | carried as explicit knowledge/reference governance |

The short version is: **OKF standardizes the envelope; the Civilization governs
the contents.**

## The Boundary

OKF does not become a trust model, control-plane primitive, certification
authority, release authority, or substitute for EventGraph provenance. Its
permissive transport posture is useful at the boundary and dangerous if confused
with internal authority.

## Capability Read

The report reads OKF as an open transport/interchange format for agent-readable
knowledge: markdown files with YAML frontmatter, reserved-file conventions
(`index.md` / `log.md`) for generic consumers, an enrichment-agent two-pass
generation pattern, and a single-file read-only visualizer — all built for
portability, with broken-link tolerance and semantic trust deferred to producers
and consumers. That fail-open posture is both the capability and the hazard: it
makes OKF a good envelope for export and exchange, and a poor substrate for
governed influence on factory work.

## Benchmark Reality

The evaluation is a format-and-boundary read, not a measured comparison: it
characterizes OKF's structural shape and trust posture against the
Civilization's substrate and makes no quantitative claim in either direction.
There is no benchmark to audit — the artifact's verdicts are architectural
(envelope vs. contents), and any future OKF export or ingest surface would be
judged by the Civilization's own gates, not by a format-level score.

## Integration Packet

The six bounded learning opportunities named by the report, with their
Civilization implications:

| Opportunity | Civilization implication |
| --- | --- |
| OKF export projection | emit the wiki as a portable, OKF-shaped bundle without weakening internal doctrine |
| Reserved-file conventions | align exported `index.md` / `log.md` shape for generic OKF consumers |
| Enrichment-agent pattern | adapt the two-pass generation shape, but emit governed references |
| External OKF ingest boundary | accept third-party bundles only at raw trust until reviewed |
| Structural/semantic vocabulary | use OKF's own boundary language to clarify doctrine |
| Single-file visualizer | explore an air-gap-friendly read-only viewer over exported knowledge |
