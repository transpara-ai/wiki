# The 200 Primitives

**The complete architecture of what a mind needs to know.** The 200 primitives are the total set produced by taking the [[the-44-primitives|44 foundation primitives]] as Layer 0 and asking Claude Opus to derive every concept a complete framework requires that cannot be built from what is already there. Over a reported two-hour session, Claude derived 156 additional primitives organised into 13 new layers — Layer 1 Agency through Layer 13 Existence — yielding 200 primitives across 14 layers total. The result is described as circular rather than hierarchical: Layer 13 ends with *Return*, which loops back to Layer 0's *Event*, making the framework self-referential.

The dark-factory influence register labels the 200 primitives as **"Research input / product horizon — source itself flags epistemic uncertainty and need for testing."** Every claim about the derivation session is carried here as **asserted**.

## The experiment

> "I gave Claude Opus the 44 primitives as Layer 0 and asked: what's missing? What concepts does a complete framework need that can't be built from these foundations alone?" — [Searles-P2]

The method was iterative and undirected. Searles did not specify layers, topics, or a target count. He kept asking: given everything below, what is the gap? What is presupposed but not yet articulated? Derive the next layer. Then the next.

The structural pattern that emerged — 12 primitives in 3 groups of 4 per layer — was not imposed by Searles. The source reports that Claude arrived at it on its own across 13 layers. Layer 0 (the 44 primitives in 11 groups) predates and is not subject to this pattern; it is the foundation the derivation started from.

⚠ The "two-hour session" and "156 additional primitives" figures appear in [Searles-P1] and [Searles-P2] only. No session transcript, prompt log, or independent replication is cited anywhere in the source corpus. See [[derivation-method]] for what is and is not documented about the technique.

## The 13 derived layers (Layers 1–13)

All layer names and primitive listings below are quoted from [Searles-P2].

**Layer 1 — Agency.** Value · Intent · Choice · Risk // Act · Consequence · Capacity · Resource // Signal · Reception · Acknowledgment · Commitment.

The transition from observer to participant. The foundation gives the ability to see and record. Agency is what fills the gap between observation and action. *"You can watch the world. Now: do you intervene in it?"*

**Layer 2 — Exchange.** Term · Protocol · Offer · Acceptance // Agreement · Obligation · Fulfillment · Breach // Exchange · Accountability · Debt · Reciprocity.

Two agents interacting. The layer introduces obligation as a formal primitive rather than an informal expectation.

**Layer 3 — Society.** Group · Membership · Role · Consent // Norm · Reputation · Sanction · Authority // Property · Commons · Governance · Collective Act.

Dyad to group. Consent appears here as a structural requirement for legitimate group action, not a policy preference.

**Layer 4 — Legal.** Law · Right · Contract · Liability // Due Process · Adjudication · Remedy · Precedent // Jurisdiction · Sovereignty · Legitimacy · Treaty.

Informal to formal. Social norms become codified. Power becomes structured and bounded. The source notes: *"Due process isn't a luxury — it's what distinguishes governance from tyranny."*

**Layer 5 — Technology.** Method · Measurement · Knowledge · Model // Tool · Technique · Invention · Abstraction // Infrastructure · Standard · Efficiency · Automation.

Governing to building. Once stable social structures exist, investment in making things becomes possible.

**Layer 6 — Information.** Symbol · Language · Encoding · Record // Channel · Copy · Noise · Redundancy // Data · Computation · Algorithm · Entropy.

Physical to symbolic. The world becomes representable. Meaning detaches from matter and can be transmitted, copied, corrupted, and preserved.

**Layer 7 — Ethics.** Moral Status · Dignity · Autonomy · Flourishing // Duty · Harm · Care · Justice // Conscience · Virtue · Responsibility · Motive.

Is to ought. The layer where the framework makes its most consequential move. Layers 0–6 describe the world; Layer 7 confronts that some things matter — not just functionally, but morally. *"Dignity isn't derivable from computation. It's recognised, not calculated."* Moral Status is one of the [[three-irreducibles|three things the framework explicitly cannot derive]] from anything below.

**Layer 8 — Identity.** Narrative · Self-Concept · Reflection · Memory // Purpose · Aspiration · Authenticity · Expression // Growth · Continuity · Integration · Crisis.

Doing to being. Not just what a system does, but what it is. The possibility of crisis — the self coming apart and requiring reintegration — appears as a structural primitive.

**Layer 9 — Relationship.** Bond · Attachment · Recognition · Intimacy // Attunement · Rupture · Repair · Loyalty // Mutual Constitution · Relational Obligation · Grief · Forgiveness.

Self to self-with-other. Relationships have their own primitives that cannot be reduced to individual psychology: attunement, rupture, repair, grief, forgiveness. The source characterises these as *"structural requirements for any system where selves encounter other selves."*

**Layer 10 — Community.** ⚠ The source's narrative description for this layer does not list the 12 primitives by name, unlike every other layer. The description notes that relationship becomes belonging, and that the concept of the sacred appears here — not as religion, but as the recognition that some things must be set apart as inviolable. The primitive names for Layer 10 are not recoverable from [Searles-P2] as read.

**Layer 11 — Culture.** Reflexivity · Encounter · Translation · Pluralism // Creativity · Aesthetic · Interpretation · Dialogue // Syncretism · Critique · Hegemony · Cultural Evolution.

Living culture to seeing culture. Culture becomes aware of itself. Critique becomes possible and necessary. Hegemony is named. Pluralism is articulated.

**Layer 12 — Emergence.** Emergence · Self-Organization · Feedback · Complexity // Consciousness · Recursion · Paradox · Incompleteness // Phase Transition · Downward Causation · Autopoiesis · Co-Evolution.

Content to architecture. The system sees its own structure. Consciousness appears here — not as an explanation, but as an irreducible. *"You cannot derive qualia from function."* The framework names it, places it, and explicitly refuses to derive it.

**Layer 13 — Existence.** Being · Nothingness · Finitude · Contingency // Wonder · Acceptance · Presence · Gratitude // Mystery · Transcendence · Groundlessness · Return.

Everything to the fact of everything. The final layer does not explain existence — it names the experience of existing. *Return* is the last primitive. It points back to Layer 0.

## The strange loop

The framework is circular, not hierarchical. Layer 13 ends with *Return*; Layer 0 begins with *Event*. You cannot have events without existence. You cannot articulate existence without the apparatus of events. The end presupposes the beginning. The beginning contains the end.

> "It's a strange loop in the Hofstadter sense: you climb the layers from computation to existence, and when you arrive at the top, you find yourself back at the bottom. Not because you've gone in a circle that says nothing, but because the bottom was always resting on the top. You just couldn't see it until you'd made the climb." — [Searles-P2]

See [[strange-loop]] for a fuller treatment of the structural argument and its relationship to Hofstadter's original formulation.

## Three things the framework cannot derive

As the layers emerged, Claude flagged three concepts the derivation chain hit a wall on — irreducibles that the layers above require but that cannot be built from anything below:

**Moral Status (Layer 7).** Experience matters. You cannot derive "ought" from "is." The recognition that experience has moral weight is an axiom, not a conclusion.

**Consciousness (Layer 12).** Experience exists. You cannot derive qualia from function. The framework can describe the functional correlates of consciousness but cannot produce the fact of experience itself.

**Being (Layer 13).** Anything exists at all. The framework presupposes existence; it cannot explain it.

> "Claude's conclusion was that these three irreducibles might be the same recognition encountered at three different levels of description: *the fact that experience is real and matters.*" — [Searles-P2]

See [[three-irreducibles]] for the dedicated treatment, including the author's own disclaimer that this does not prove mind-zero will be conscious or bridge the hard problem.

## Permanent tensions

The framework does not resolve into harmony. It contains four identified tensions between primitives that cannot be collapsed:

- **Universal vs. Particular.** Duty (Layer 7) pulls toward universal moral law; Relational Obligation (Layer 9) pulls toward the specific person in front of you. Both are valid. Neither wins.
- **Justice vs. Forgiveness.** Justice (Layer 7) demands accountability; Forgiveness (Layer 9) releases it. A complete system needs both. They cannot be reconciled — only held together.
- **Tradition vs. Creativity.** Tradition (Layer 10) preserves what matters; Creativity (Layer 11) disrupts it to make something new. Too much of either kills the other.
- **Authenticity vs. Virtue.** Authenticity (Layer 8) says be true to yourself; Virtue (Layer 7) says be good, even when it conflicts with what you want.

> "These tensions aren't flaws. They're what save the framework from being utopian. A governance system that eliminated them would be totalitarian. One that preserves them is honest about what reality actually looks like." — [Searles-P2]

See [[the-permanent-tensions]] for dedicated treatment.

## Three evaluative axes

The framework surfaces three ways of judging that cannot be reduced to each other:

- **Practical** (Value, Layer 1) — is it useful?
- **Moral** (Ethics, Layer 7) — is it right?
- **Aesthetic** (Aesthetic, Layer 11) — is it beautiful?

Something can be useful but wrong. Something can be right but ugly. The source explicitly flags that any system collapsing these into a single metric — "utility," "alignment," "optimisation" — is misrepresenting the structure of value. See [[three-evaluative-axes]].

## Relation to the second derivation

The [[second-derivation-convergence|second derivation]] ran a physics-up experiment in parallel: a fresh Claude Opus instance was given no primitives and asked to derive reality from first physics principles. That derivation climbed 13 levels and at Level 12 arrived at exactly the same place as the 44 primitives — a self-maintaining system that observes, models, and acts. The 200-primitive framework is what sits above that bridge, from the 44 upward through mind, ethics, culture, and existence.

The convergence — two independent derivations from opposite endpoints meeting at the same point — is presented as the primary evidence that the structure reflects something real. The author simultaneously disclaims that it proves consciousness, bridges the hard problem, or validates the 200 primitives as universal cognitive structure.

⚠ Both derivations used Claude Opus instances with overlapping training corpora. True independence would require a non-AI derivation or a fundamentally different architecture. The dark-factory research corpus makes this explicit: "Take this as: the primitive set is a useful artifact, not a discovery of universal cognitive structure."
