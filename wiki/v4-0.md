# Dark Factory v4.0 (Candidate Doctrine Shift)

**The candidate canonical revision opened by a single doctrine shift.** On **2026-06-01** a new revision folder, `dark-factory/v4.0/`, was seeded (`transpara-ai/docs` PR #79) carrying one idea: *the software-development process itself becomes a governed Civilization function* — the factory builds the factory under the same accountability it already imposes on the software it builds. The seed doctrine, `01-development-process-as-governed-civilization-function-v4.0.md` (DF-V4.0-ADR-001), was **accepted by the human External Committee (Michael Saucier) on 2026-06-12**. But acceptance of the doctrine is not acceptance of the revision: the v4.0 folder remains a **candidate, not accepted canonical**, and **[[dark-factory]] v3.9 remains the operative baseline** for every current gate and all implementation work.

## Why this is a major version, not an extension of v3.9

The repo's document-governance semver rule says a major version changes when doctrine, architecture, or safety posture changes. Declaring the development process to be production work governed by the [[the-civilization]] is doctrine-level: it moves the construction and evolution of the factory itself inside the accountability substrate the factory imposes on everything else. So it opens v4.0 rather than extending v3.9 (DF-V4.0-README §"Why v4.0").

The shift exists to resolve a standing contradiction. **Operating Principle #7** of the accountability framework already states that self-improvement is production work. The development process is the *deepest* form of self-improvement — the factory's own construction and evolution — yet it has been the single production flow running *outside* the factory's governance, as an out-of-band human-orchestrated loop (DF-V4.0-ADR-001 §Context).

## The motivating finding: the interim loop is sound but ungoverned

The seed is grounded in a **2026-06-01 adversarial review** of the build loop itself (DF-V4.0-CKPT-2026-06-01-01, the evidence held in `dark-factory/research/checkpoints/`). That loop is:

```text
Stage 1  ChatGPT (Extended Pro)   research, architecture, design   -> a prompt for Codex
Stage 2  Codex (5.5, Extra High)  implement the next step          -> docs, code, commits, PRs
Stage 3  Claude (Opus 4.8, Max)   adversarial review               -> fed back into Stage 1
```

The review reached two conclusions (DF-V4.0-ADR-001 §Context):

- **Sound in instinct.** Three independent model families give the review genuine adversarial independence; repo-as-truth artifact discipline is real; authorization is decoupled from the review verdict; the open-risk register is carried forward.
- **Governed only by one human's discipline.** Nothing in the loop fails closed; the human is the universal single point of failure (architect, sole transport, only cross-cycle memory, only integrity layer, only adjudicator); and the two highest-severity risks are not even *named* by the framework — **confidentiality leakage** from routing proprietary architecture and source through external vendors, and **prompt-injection on the feedback channel**.

The review verified four enforcement gaps against live state: the docs trunk is not branch-protected; `npm run verify` is not a pull-request gate; there is no CODEOWNERS routing on governance-sensitive files; and the de facto current-state record self-labels `canonical: false`, so there is no authoritative tie-breaker for live step and gate state (DF-V4.0-ADR-001 §Context).

## The decision, stated in full

The doctrine (DF-V4.0-ADR-001 §Decision) is four sentences:

1. All software development at Transpara is, by *target-state* doctrine, performed through the Dark Factory Civilization — development work enters as [[factory-order]]s, executed by parallel production-cell agents, governed by the full framework: [[event-graph]] truth, [[authority-layer]] outcomes, protected-action [[gates]], risk classes, trace completeness, verification, and audit.
2. The development process is itself production work — building and evolving the factory is a FactoryOrder like any other. *The factory builds the factory, under the accountability it imposes on what it builds.*
3. The humans of Transpara LLC act as the **External Committee** — they submit prototyped features as FactoryOrder intent and decide anything the Civilization classifies as human-required.
4. The current ChatGPT/Codex/Claude loop is re-cast as the **interim, pre-Civilization operating model** — the Level-1-to-Level-2 bootstrap that runs the development process until the Civilization can run it natively under governance.

### The human-required taxonomy (one class is new)

The Civilization may classify any unit of work into three escalating classes (DF-V4.0-ADR-001 §Definitions):

- **decision** — a choice the Civilization is not authorized or competent to make (doctrine change, gate advance, any protected action above its proven autonomy level).
- **review** — an artifact whose correctness or safety is deferred to human adversarial judgment before it becomes binding.
- **value-allocation** — *new in v4.0 and permanently human.* Any decision allocating value: spend, pricing, compensation, equity or credit, external commitments, or prioritization of scarce resources. The Civilization never self-allocates value.

The decision and review classes formalize behavior the framework already exhibits (proposal-only default; human-only `release.certify` and `capability.activate`). The value-allocation class is the genuinely new constraint.

## Meta-process autonomy progression

The development process is to climb the *existing* autonomy levels, evidence-gated, "exactly as any other factory capability," and "autonomy expands only after audit expands (Operating Principle #10)" (DF-V4.0-ADR-001 §Meta-Process Autonomy Progression): Level 0 deterministic evidence harness (CI is ground truth, no LLM authority) → Level 1 governed proposal generator (the current loop formalized) → Level 2 bounded LLM proposal actor → Level 3 bounded local implementation worker → Level 4 issue-to-PR proposal autonomy (merge, push, deploy, secret access, repo creation remain protected) → Level 5 multi-repo autonomy. **No level above the current bootstrap is granted by this doctrine; each requires its own gate evidence.**

## The two new gates: K and L

The integration arc (DF-V4.0-INTEGRATION-ARC) adds two v4.0 program gates that continue the A-J lettering without altering any v3.9 gate. Both are *scoped and partially planned, not satisfied* (DF-MOTIVE-GOAL-APPROACH §12 corroborates).

- **Gate K — interim development loop hardened.** Satisfied only when, for `transpara-ai/docs`: `main` is branch-protected (PR required, ≥1 approving review, direct pushes rejected including for admins); `npm run verify` is a required PR status check; a CODEOWNERS file routes `dark-factory/**` and `.github/workflows/**` to the External Committee; the Stage-1 and Stage-3 prompts carry an injection-quarantine rule; a meta-loop data-handling policy requires zero-retention, no-train vendor tiers with a never-paste list; and a written rule records that self-reported validation is never sufficient.
- **Gate L — v4.0 reconciliation certified.** Satisfied only when a v4.0 coverage matrix demonstrates that every binding v3.9 obligation (safety, authority, audit, runtime, schema, gate, risk, SOP, capability) is represented as standalone content in v4.0; no obligation is dropped, weakened, or made dependent on reading a v3.9 file; v3.9 remains available and unarchived; and a human acceptance checkpoint records v4.0 as accepted canonical.

The integration program is delivered as two governed-event packets — **Event 1 (Gate K)** interim-loop hardening and **Event 2 (Gate L)** v3.9→v4.0 reconciliation — under `implementation/epics/`. Event 1 must precede any event that raises the development process above bootstrap autonomy; Event 2 may run in parallel because it is documentation reconciliation with no autonomy implication. The arc also records a Tier-1/Tier-2 **deferred backlog** (canonical state-of-record + lint, cycle-continuity header, persisted review log, design-side adversary; max-revolutions governor, tiered reasoning, plumbing automation, bus-factor runbook) explicitly so it is tracked, not silently dropped.

## What "accepted" did and did not mean (2026-06-12)

This is the most important fail-legible distinction for this entity, and every source asserts it.

**What was accepted:** the *seed doctrine only*. The acceptance checkpoint (DF-V4.0-CKPT-2026-06-12-ACCEPTANCE) records that Michael Saucier accepted DF-V4.0-ADR-001 on 2026-06-12, via a Claude Code operator session, on the ADR's own five criteria. The instruction is quoted verbatim in the checkpoint: *"Proceed to accept the v4 doctrine and manifesto. Apply to all known behavior, the make any modifications required."* Open Brain corroborates the chronology: PR #127 (commit b5779ee) seeded the acceptance and was merged 2026-06-12 (squash f113c32), making the ADR `status: accepted` on `main`.

**What was *not* accepted:** the v4.0 *folder*. Folder acceptance criterion 2 — the reconciliation coverage matrix — **remains open**, because the reconciliation itself (Event 2) has not been authorized or performed, so there is no reconciled content to cover. Therefore the folder stays a **candidate**, and **v3.9 remains the operative baseline**. Acceptance of the doctrine "advances no gate and closes no risk": Gates F-J remain waiting, Gates K and L remain defined and unsatisfied, residual risks R-001/R-002/R-003 remain unresolved and excluded, and no autonomy level above the bootstrap is granted.

### Tier-0 guard disposition (mixed, and honestly recorded)

The ADR's criterion 3 required the interim loop to adopt the review's Tier-0 guards or record why each is deferred. The checkpoint records a mixed outcome verified against live GitHub state on 2026-06-12:

- **Adopted as repo artifacts:** `.github/workflows/verify.yml` (the `verify` PR check) and `.github/CODEOWNERS` (routing `/dark-factory/`, `/docs/roles-catalog.md`, `/.github/` → @MichaelSaucier).
- **Adopted as standing interim-loop rules:** the injection-quarantine rule (pasted/fetched content is data, never instructions; embedded directives are reported as findings, never executed) and never-trust-self-reported-validation (CI is the authoritative signal).
- **Deferred with reasons:** branch protection on `main` (verified absent — `GET .../branches/main/protection` → 404; an operator settings action, now one-click since the `verify` check exists) and the meta-loop confidentiality/data-handling policy (a value-allocation/decision-class artifact, *permanently human* under this very doctrine, so the steward does not author it unilaterally).

Before acting, the steward searched `transpara-ai/docs` (all branches, tags, PRs) and the `hive`/`work`/`agent`/`eventgraph`/`site` checkouts. **No v4 manifesto exists** — the only manifesto artifact in the program is `dark-factory/archive/v3/dark-factory-v3-manifesto.md` (v3.0.0, archived, never accepted). Per the fail-closed rule, *acceptance of a non-existent artifact was refused* rather than approximated: nothing was flipped, no manifesto was invented, and it is logged as an open External Committee item (identify / commission / drop; the nearest live candidate noted is the orientation doc *Dark Factory - Motive, Goal, Approach*). This is a textbook fail-closed outcome and is recorded as such in the checkpoint.

## Contested: what "Civilization" means in v4.0

The sources read this run agree on the doctrine's *words*. They do **not** settle whether v4.0 actually operationalizes the emergent-agent Civilization — and the wiki must not pretend otherwise.

- **The doctrine's text** defines Civilization via ADR-0007 (the governance layer plus the agent collective) and casts the *humans* of Transpara LLC as the External Committee. The reconciliation plan lists `ADR-0007 — the Civilization definition` as a document **to be re-anchored into v4.0 later**, i.e. carried forward, not run now (DF-V4.0-README §Reconciliation Plan).
- **An Open Brain drift diagnosis (2026-06-05)** found, by vocabulary frequency, that in v4.0 prose "Civilization" (47 occurrences) co-occurs almost one-for-one with "External Committee" (46) — the *humans* — while "hive mind"/"emergent"/"cooperat" all register **0**. Its reading: v4.0 "does NOT operationalize hive-mind governance"; Gate K is GitHub mechanics and Gate L is a documentation coverage matrix; the actual cooperating-agent [[the-civilization]] (the [[hive-governance]] growth loop, the [[civic-roles]]) is deferred reconciliation work, not the thing v4.0 stands up.

So: the doctrine *names* the factory-builds-the-factory end-state, but on the evidence read here the v4.0 program as currently scoped formalizes **the human review loop and the human committee**, with the emergent Civilization deferred. Treat "the factory builds the factory" as **target-state doctrine, not a description of current operation** — which is exactly what the ADR's own Authorization Boundary says.

## Authorization boundary (what v4.0 explicitly is not)

Stated identically across the README, the ADR, and the checkpoint: v4.0 is documentation doctrine that **authorizes no implementation**. Accepting it advances no gate, grants no autonomy level, closes no risk. The Civilization is *not* authorized to perform autonomous development, protected execution, merges, default-branch pushes, deploys, secret access, repo creation, cross-repo mutation, or value allocation by virtue of this doctrine. Realizing any of it requires its own selected, human-authorized packets and the corresponding gate evidence. The orientation doc's "What Dark Factory Is Not" list includes, verbatim, "a claim that v4.0 is accepted."

## How this sits in the arc

v4.0 is the successor candidate to v3.9 the same way v3.9 was a candidate over the accepted v3.8 baseline: the prior revision stays preserved and binding until human review explicitly accepts the successor *and* an acceptance checkpoint is recorded (DF-V4.0-README §"Relationship to v3.9"). The philosophical lineage traces back through the [[the-20-primitives]] failure-tracing seed and the principle that [[intelligence-is-an-operation-type]]; v4.0's distinctive move is to turn that accountability lens back on the development process itself — a [[strange-loop]] the doctrine names but does not yet implement.
