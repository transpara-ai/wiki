# Sakana AI Adjacent Technologies and Organisations

**Sakana AI Adjacent Technologies and Organisations** extends
[[sakana-ai-evaluation|the first Sakana report]] outward into the research
ecosystem around Sakana AI: collaborators, recursive self-improvement work,
autonomous research systems, evolutionary AI tooling, and collective
intelligence orchestration.

## Method

The report records an adversarial deep-research workflow:

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Agents | 108 |
| Sources fetched | 26 |
| Claims extracted | 120 |
| Claims verified | 25 |
| Claims refuted | 12 |

Claims are carried as high confidence, medium confidence, refuted, or known-gap
evidence. That verification posture is part of the material value of the report:
the false or overclaimed leads remain visible rather than disappearing.

## Primary Map

The report frames Sakana AI as sitting at the intersection of:

- evolutionary computation;
- autonomous research systems;
- recursive self-improving coding agents;
- multi-agent and multi-model orchestration;
- nature-inspired AI tooling.

The closest named research collaborator is Jeff Clune's lab at UBC / Vector
Institute, tied to both the Darwin Godel Machine and the AI Scientist line.

## Refuted Claims Matter

The report explicitly preserves claims killed by the verification process,
including overclaims around DARWIN, FutureHouse Robin, InternAgent-1.5, and
specific benchmark numbers. This matters for the wiki because the research corpus
is not just a list of conclusions; it is a provenance record of which claims
survived contact with adversarial checking.

## Boundary

This is a companion research map, not a doctrine change. It does not make Sakana
AI, Jeff Clune's lab, EvoJAX, EvoScientist, or Together AI part of the
Civilization architecture. It identifies external systems that may later be
evaluated through a governed research-to-doctrine path.
