# The Fourteen Layers (Layer 0–13 Stack)

**The vertical spine of the 200-primitive ontology.** On **2026-02-28**, in the second post of the arc, *"From 44 to 200,"* Matt Searles (working with Claude) recorded the result of a derivation experiment: starting from the [[the-44-primitives]] as "Layer 0," Claude Opus was asked, layer by layer, *what's missing?* — and over roughly two hours produced **156 additional primitives across 13 new layers**, each derived from gaps in the one below it. Layer 0 plus those 13 derived layers make **fourteen layers** (numbered 0–13), holding **200 primitives** in total (44 + 156). This article is the *vertical* organising axis of the framework; the rungs of the *quantitative* ladder are documented in [[the-primitives]] (20 → 44 → 200), and the individual sets in [[the-20-primitives]], [[the-44-primitives]], and [[the-200-primitives]].

The stack runs from computation to existence: **Layer 0 Foundation** (Event, EventStore, Clock, Hash, Self) up through **Agency, Exchange, Society, Legal, Technology, Information, Ethics, Identity, Relationship, Community, Culture, Emergence**, to **Layer 13 Existence** ([Searles-P2], [Searles-P5]). The top and bottom curve back to meet each other — Layer 13 ends with the primitive *Return*, Layer 0 begins with *Event* — which is the subject of [[strange-loop]].

> ⚠ **Fail-legible note (the whole structure is asserted, not proven).** The 14 layers are the output of *one* AI instance deriving from a fixed prompt, not an independently reproduced result. The author's own audit post rates the framework's soundness uncertain, and the series' epistemic guardrail [[the-cult-test]] insists the framework is "a tool, not a truth." [[dark-factory]] adopts "200 primitives / 14 layers" only as **research input / product horizon**, never as implementation scope or proven ontology. Everything below is reported as the source presents it, and labelled where the source itself hedges.

## How the layers were derived

The method is stated plainly in [Searles-P2]. Claude Opus was given the 44 primitives as Layer 0 and asked a single repeated question: *given everything below, what's the gap? What's presupposed but not yet articulated?* Searles says he "didn't tell it where to go… didn't suggest layers or topics," only "kept asking: derive the next layer. Then the next. Then the next."

Two structural regularities are reported as **emergent**, not imposed:

- Each new layer "emerged from gaps in the one below it" — the layers are derivationally ordered, each presupposing the ones beneath.
- Each derived layer "contained exactly 12 primitives in 3 groups of 4 — a structure the model arrived at on its own, not one I imposed" ([Searles-P2]).

> ⚠ **Fail-legible note (emergence of the 3×4 shape).** That the 12-in-3-groups pattern arose unprompted is the author's testimony about a session he ran; there is no run log or independent reproduction in the corpus. Treat the regularity as a reported observation, not a verified property.

## What "12 in 3 groups of 4" does and doesn't cover

The one-line framing of this entity ("12 primitives in 3 groups of 4") is accurate **for the 13 derived layers** and is the wrong count **for Layer 0**:

- **Layers 1–13** each hold 12 primitives, arranged in 3 groups of 4. This is visible in the "The Layers" listing in [Searles-P2], e.g. Layer 1 Agency: *Value, Intent, Choice, Risk · Act, Consequence, Capacity, Resource · Signal, Reception, Acknowledgment, Commitment* — three groups of four.
- **Layer 0 (Foundation)** is **not** a 12-in-3-groups layer. It is the **44-primitive foundation in 11 groups** that the derivation *started from* ([Searles-P2], "The 44"). 44 + (13 × 12) = 44 + 156 = **200**.

So "14 layers, 200 primitives, 12 in 3 groups of 4" only adds up once you treat Layer 0 as the special 44-primitive base and the 3×4 rule as describing the 13 layers built on top of it.

## Layer 0 has five primitives in its Foundation group, not four

The brief's one-line names Layer 0 Foundation as "Event, Hash, Clock, Self" (four). Both posts that give the Foundation group give **five**: **Event · EventStore · Clock · Hash · Self** ([Searles-P2] "The 44"; [Searles-P6] "The Walk"). The gloss in the source: *"Something happens. It's recorded. Time passes. Records are tamper-proof. There's a 'me' doing the recording."* The companion article [[strange-loop]] also lists the five-primitive form. This article follows the source: **EventStore is part of the Foundation group**, and the four-item version is an undercount.

(Note that "Foundation" is doing double duty in the corpus: it is both the name of **Layer 0 as a whole** — the 44 primitives in 11 groups — and the name of the **first 5-primitive group within** that layer. The five primitives above are the group; the 44 are the layer.)

## The fourteen layers

Below is the stack as given in [Searles-P2]'s "The Layers" listing, with each layer's transition slogan and, where the source supplies them, the three groups of four. **Layer 4 appears as "Legal" here and as "Law" in [Searles-P5]** — a naming variant flagged, not silently reconciled. Two layers in the listing (3 was abbreviated in places; **Layer 10 Community is given with no primitive list at all** in "The Layers") are reported as they appear; the missing items are marked.

- **Layer 0 — Foundation.** *The 44 primitives in 11 groups (Foundation, Causality, Identity, Expectations, Trust, Confidence, Instrumentation, Query, Integrity, Deception, Health).* The ability to see and record: what makes anything recordable and tamper-evident. This is the [[event-graph]] realised as an ontology — append-only, hash-chained, causally linked. *(Composition from [Searles-P2] "The 44"; see [[the-44-primitives]].)*

- **Layer 1 — Agency.** Value, Intent, Choice, Risk · Act, Consequence, Capacity, Resource · Signal, Reception, Acknowledgment, Commitment. *Observer → participant:* "You can watch the world. Now: do you intervene in it?"

- **Layer 2 — Exchange.** Term, Protocol, Offer, Acceptance · Agreement, Obligation, Fulfillment, Breach · Exchange, Accountability, Debt, Reciprocity. *Individual → dyad:* what happens when two actors transact, negotiate, or collaborate.

- **Layer 3 — Society.** Group, Membership, Role, Consent · Norm, Reputation, Sanction, Authority · Property, Commons, Governance, Collective Act. *Dyad → group:* identity within a collective, rules beyond individual agreements; "Consent appears here… as a structural requirement for legitimate group action."

- **Layer 4 — Legal.** Law, Right, Contract, Liability · Due Process, Adjudication, Remedy, Precedent · Jurisdiction, Sovereignty, Legitimacy, Treaty. *Informal → formal:* social norms become codified; "Due process… is what distinguishes governance from tyranny." *(Named "Law" in [Searles-P5].)*

- **Layer 5 — Technology.** Method, Measurement, Knowledge, Model · Tool, Technique, Invention, Abstraction · Infrastructure, Standard, Efficiency, Automation. *Governing → building:* the primitives of engineering and science.

- **Layer 6 — Information.** Symbol, Language, Encoding, Record · Channel, Copy, Noise, Redundancy · Data, Computation, Algorithm, Entropy. *Physical → symbolic:* "the layer where meaning detaches from matter and can be transmitted, copied, corrupted, and preserved."

- **Layer 7 — Ethics.** Moral Status, Dignity, Autonomy, Flourishing · Duty, Harm, Care, Justice · Conscience, Virtue, Responsibility, Motive. *Is → ought:* "the framework makes its most consequential move… Dignity isn't derivable from computation. It's recognised, not calculated." Holds the irreducible **Moral Status** (see [[three-irreducibles]]).

- **Layer 8 — Identity.** Narrative, Self-Concept, Reflection, Memory · Purpose, Aspiration, Authenticity, Expression · Growth, Continuity, Integration, Crisis. *Doing → being:* "not just what a system does, but what it *is*."

- **Layer 9 — Relationship.** Bond, Attachment, Recognition, Intimacy · Attunement, Rupture, Repair, Loyalty · Mutual Constitution, Relational Obligation, Grief, Forgiveness. *Self → self-with-other:* "Grief. Forgiveness. These aren't luxuries. They're structural requirements for any system where selves encounter other selves."

- **Layer 10 — Community.** *No primitive list is given for this layer in "The Layers."* *Relationship → belonging:* "The concept of the sacred appears… the recognition that some things are set apart, treated as inviolable." *(Primitive list THIN — absent from [Searles-P2]'s listing as read this run; the prose names the Sacred primitive, and [Searles-P9] "The Cult Test" treats "Sacred (Layer 10)" as a primitive, but the full 12 were not enumerated in the sources read.)*

- **Layer 11 — Culture.** Reflexivity, Encounter, Translation, Pluralism · Creativity, Aesthetic, Interpretation, Dialogue · Syncretism, Critique, Hegemony, Cultural Evolution. *Living culture → seeing culture:* "Culture becomes aware of itself… This is where critique becomes possible."

- **Layer 12 — Emergence.** Emergence, Self-Organization, Feedback, Complexity · Consciousness, Recursion, Paradox, Incompleteness · Phase Transition, Downward Causation, Autopoiesis, Co-Evolution. *Content → architecture:* "The system sees its own structure." Holds the irreducible **Consciousness** — "you cannot derive qualia from function" (see [[three-irreducibles]]).

- **Layer 13 — Existence.** Being, Nothingness, Finitude, Contingency · Wonder, Acceptance, Presence, Gratitude · Mystery, Transcendence, Groundlessness, Return. *Everything → the fact of everything:* "The final layer doesn't explain existence. It names the *experience* of existing." Holds the irreducible **Being**, and ends with **Return** — the loop back to Layer 0.

## Each layer derived from a gap in the one below

The ordering is not arbitrary grouping; it is a chain of presupposition. The source describes the climb as a sequence of transitions, each layer answering a "what's presupposed but not yet there?" gap left by the layer beneath ([Searles-P2]):

observer → participant (0→1) · individual → dyad (1→2) · dyad → group (2→3) · informal → formal (3→4) · governing → building (4→5) · physical → symbolic (5→6) · **is → ought** (6→7) · doing → being (7→8) · self → self-with-other (8→9) · relationship → belonging (9→10) · living culture → seeing culture (10→11) · content → architecture (11→12) · everything → the fact of everything (12→13).

The is→ought step (Layer 6 to Layer 7) is the one the author calls the framework's "most consequential move": the point where description stops and the recognition that *some things matter morally* enters — a recognition the framework explicitly refuses to derive from the layers below it.

## The loop, the irreducibles, and the tensions

Three structural features of the stack are documented at length elsewhere in the corpus and are summarised here only to place them:

- **The strange loop.** The stack "isn't a tower. It's a circle." Layer 13 ends with *Return*; Layer 0 begins with *Event*; each presupposes the other, so the structure rests on itself rather than on a foundation. Full treatment: [[strange-loop]].
- **The three irreducibles.** As the layers emerged, three concepts appeared that the framework "explicitly could not derive from anything below them": **Moral Status** (Layer 7), **Consciousness** (Layer 12), and **Being** (Layer 13) — claimed by Claude to "might be the same recognition encountered at three different levels of description." Full treatment, with the is–ought caveats: [[three-irreducibles]].
- **Permanent tensions and three evaluative axes.** [Searles-P2] also notes the stack does not resolve into harmony — it holds irreducible tensions across layers (Universal vs. Particular: Duty L7 vs. Relational Obligation L9; Justice vs. Forgiveness: L7 vs. L9; Tradition vs. Creativity: L10 vs. L11; Authenticity vs. Virtue: L8 vs. L7) and three non-collapsible ways of judging (Practical/Value L1, Moral/Ethics L7, Aesthetic/Aesthetic L11). These are reported here as further structure of the same stack; they have no dedicated article yet.

## The walk: each layer as a real-world application

Two later posts "walk" the same fourteen layers for different purposes, and both are *applications* of the stack rather than redefinitions of it.

In **"Fourteen Layers, A Hundred Problems"** ([Searles-P6], "The Walk"), each layer is paired with a problem the [[event-graph]] could address — Layer 0 *Verifiable History*, Layer 1 *Persistent Memory*, Layer 2 *Two-Party Trust*, Layer 3 *Group Governance*, Layer 4 *Compliance and Dispute Resolution*, Layer 5 *Software and Supply Chains*, Layer 6 *Content Provenance*, Layer 7 *AI Alignment*, Layer 8 *Reputation and Credentials*, Layer 9 *Social Networks*, Layer 10 *Platform Governance*, Layer 11 *Creative Attribution*, Layer 12 *Self-Improving Systems*, Layer 13 *Living Constitutions*. The author's framing: the 200-primitive framework "wasn't designed as a taxonomy of industries… but first principles, if they're actually right, have a habit of mapping onto the real world."

In **"The Weight"** ([Searles-P27], 2026-03-03), the same layers are walked a third time as a layer-by-layer catalogue of human suffering ("This post is about blood"), closing with the same loop: "the children of the dead inherit the same cascade. Layer 13 feeds Layer 1." This post is the emotional counterpart to the structural argument.

## Two different things both called "the layers"

A reader cross-referencing posts will hit a genuine naming hazard, and the wiki states it rather than papering over it. There are **two parallel layer vocabularies** in the corpus:

1. **The primitive-stack names** (this article's subject, from [Searles-P2] / [Searles-P5]): 0 Foundation, 1 Agency, 2 Exchange, 3 Society, 4 Legal/Law, 5 Technology, 6 Information, 7 Ethics, 8 Identity, 9 Relationship, 10 Community, 11 Culture, 12 Emergence, 13 Existence.
2. **The thirteen-graphs product names** (used in [Searles-P6]'s "What this touches" applications, in [Searles-P27], and in the per-graph deep-dive posts): Work, Market, Social, Justice, Research, Knowledge, Ethics, Identity, Relationship/Population, Community, Governance, Culture, Existence. See [[thirteen-graphs]].

> ⚠ **Fail-legible note (the two schemes do NOT line up one-to-one).** The "Layer N" headers in [Searles-P27] use the *product* names and renumber against the *primitive* stack. Concretely, in "The Weight": **Layer 11 = Governance** and **Layer 12 = Culture** — the reverse of the primitive stack, where **Layer 11 = Culture** and **Layer 12 = Emergence** (Governance is not a primitive-stack layer name at all). Likewise [Searles-P27] folds Research+Knowledge into "Layers 5 and 6" and uses "Markets/Justice/Society" where the primitive stack says "Exchange/Legal/Society." Do not read a "Layer 12" reference in one post as the same layer as "Layer 12" in another without checking which vocabulary is in use. The primitive-stack numbering in this article is the one tied to the 200 primitives; the thirteen-graphs numbering is a product mapping over the same substrate.

## Standing of the framework (research input, not proven ontology)

The fourteen-layer stack is **philosophy and product horizon in this corpus, not shipped scope.** Three load-bearing caveats, each from a source read this run:

- **It is an AI derivation from a single prompt.** [Searles-P2] is explicit that Claude produced the layers; the author's audit post concedes the derivation is AI-on-AI and rates the framework's soundness uncertain ([[the-primitives]] carries the audit detail).
- **The author built a guardrail against believing it.** [[the-cult-test]] states the framework "is a tool, not a truth," that "the scientific method is the authority layer," and that "the framework must not become sacred" — applying the Sacred primitive (Layer 10) to the framework itself is named as the failure mode.
- **Dark Factory consumes it narrowly.** The first-party influence register classes "200 primitives / 14 layers" as **research input / product horizon** that "provides a high-level map for product graph adjacency and long-range Dark Factory implications," and lists "whether the 200 primitives are a true ontology or useful map" as an **open question** ([[dark-factory]], Decision Register / Open Issues). It is never admitted as implementation authority.
